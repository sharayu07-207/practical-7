<h2>About</h2>
<p>
Born as Manikarnika Tambe, Rani Laxmi Bai was one of the leading figures of the Indian Rebellion of 1857.
 She became a symbol of resistance against British rule in India.
 Known for her bravery, she famously fought with her infant son tied to her back, leading her army into the battle of Gwalior.
</p>

<h2>Legacy</h2>
<li><strong>Leadership:</strong> Effectively governed Jhansi and organized a volunteer army.</li>

                <li><strong>Iconic Struggle:</strong> Her life remains an inspiration for women's empowerment and patriotism in India.</li>
